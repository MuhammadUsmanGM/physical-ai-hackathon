# Foundations of Embodied Intelligence

Embodied intelligence is a core principle of Physical AI, suggesting that intelligence emerges from the interaction between an agent and its environment. This perspective fundamentally differs from traditional AI approaches that treat perception and action as separate, abstract problems.

## The Embodiment Hypothesis

The embodiment hypothesis proposes that:
- Physical form shapes cognitive processes
- Sensorimotor experiences provide the foundation for abstract reasoning
- Environmental interaction drives learning and adaptation
- Physical constraints can enhance rather than limit intelligent behavior

## Physical Reasoning

Physical AI systems must understand and reason about:
- **Dynamics**: How objects move and interact under forces
- **Kinematics**: The geometric aspects of motion without considering forces
- **Material Properties**: How different materials behave under various conditions
- **Spatial Relationships**: Understanding position, orientation, and geometry in 3D space

## Course Overview and Connection to Learning Outcomes

This course in Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics is designed to develop specific competencies:

1. **Technical Understanding**: Comprehension of how AI systems function in physical space
2. **System Integration**: Ability to connect AI models with physical control systems
